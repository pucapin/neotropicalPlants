Plants & Birds

Millions of years ago, when birds and plants became part of the great planetary community comprised of all living beings, a strong coevolutionary relationship was woven and continues to this day. Behind their tangle of stems, aromas, and leaves, foliage conceals diverse trills, feathered wings of different colors, and nests.

Species that attract birds

Tecoma Stans

Tecoma stans is a fast-growing shrub or small tree native to the Neotropical region, particularly widespread in Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America. It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family and is known for its tubular, bright yellow flowers arranged in terminal clusters.

These flowers are rich in nectar and serve as an important food source for nectarivorous birds, such as orioles and tanagers. The plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often used in reforestation and ornamental landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ecological value.

Garcinia Madruno

With its glossy green leaves and rounded yellow fruits, Garcinia madruno stands out in the lush tropical forests of Central and South America. The tree produces small, golden fruits that split open to reveal soft, snow-white pulp with a sweet, tangy flavor—often compared to a mangosteen. These eye-catching fruits are a favorite of colorful frugivorous birds like toucans and tanagers, who help spread the seeds through the forest. Thriving in humid lowlands, Garcinia madruno not only adds vibrant color to the landscape but also plays a vital role in forest ecology and regeneration.

Learn More!

Which flower looks like a beak and lures hummingbirds?

Heliconia—bright, beak-shaped, and a hummingbird favorite!

What plant feeds birds and gives us chocolate?

Cacao tree—birds eat the fruit, we get chocolate!

What tree has fruits toucans love and poop out to spread seeds?

Virola—a favorite snack and seed-spreader for toucans!

Which spiky fruit tree feeds parrots and monkeys alike?

Annona (like soursop)—soft fruit, hard seeds, bird-approved!
